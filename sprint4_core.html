Neuroplasticity

Neuroplasticity

Neuroplasticity has taught us that our mind has the capabilities to change and adapt. There are 10 principles that helps you better understand how neuroplasticity works and how it is beneficial in the way we learn and think. The principles are as followed.

  • Use it or lose it (If you do not keep doing an action your brain will forget it)
  • Use it and Improve it (If you keep doing an action you will get better at it)
  • Specificity (You need to be specific with your training or it will not be effective)
  • Repetition Matters (To make an action stick repetition is key)
  • Intensity Matters (Doing higher intensive training can be more effective)
  • Time Matters (Training at certain times can be more effective)
  • Salience Matters (Training must have meaning to the person or it will not stick)
  • Age Matters (The younger you are the easier it is to train)
  • Transference or generalization (Learning one skill can transfer to another skill)
  • Interference (Bad habits can stop good habit e.g., Procrastination)

Understanding these principles will improve your way of learning as you understand that your brain is very flexible and learning these principles will help you improve your skills.

Growth Mindset

There are two types of mindsets a fixed mindset and a growth mindset. A fixed mindset is a mindset in which you are stuck with believing you can only be this good and do not believe they can improve. These people will usually paper over their faults to avoid looking stupid. A growth mindset is a mindset that understands they have areas that can be improved and tries their best to improve it. They are continually striving to improve themselves.

This exploration has shown I have a fixed mindset when it comes to improving myself. This has help me understand my mindset and how to change it so I can strive to be better cause I know I can.
